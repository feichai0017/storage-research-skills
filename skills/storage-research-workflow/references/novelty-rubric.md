# Novelty Rubric

Use this ladder conservatively.
Score by the strongest claim the artifact can actually defend.

## Level 0: Not Publishable As Research

- mostly implementation cleanup
- mostly code quality or engineering debt reduction
- mostly rephrasing of known mechanisms

## Level 1: Design Note / Internal Memo / Blog-Quality

- useful framing
- nice architecture cleanup
- small engineering trick
- weak evidence or heavy overlap with known systems

## Level 2: Workshop / Narrow Systems Paper

- real problem
- bounded contribution
- clear but not field-shifting mechanism
- moderate evidence
- overlap with adjacent work is manageable but visible

## Level 3: Solid Conference Contribution

- clear real-world pressure
- boundary or mechanism meaningfully differs from prior work
- convincing artifact and evaluation
- contribution is more than packaging or tuning

## Level 4: Top-Tier Contender

- strong and important problem
- contribution changes how the community thinks about the boundary
- mechanism and evidence both feel hard to dismiss
- related work sharpens the distinction instead of collapsing it

## Scoring Questions

- Is the problem important enough?
- Is the claim boundary fresh enough?
- Is the mechanism non-trivial?
- Is the evidence strong enough for the ambition?
- Would a skeptical reviewer say "this is just X with a new wrapper"?
