# Venue Band Rubric

Estimate the venue band from the evidence and claim, not from ambition.

## Design Note / Tech Report / Blog

Use when:
- idea is interesting but evidence is thin
- artifact is incomplete
- overlap with prior art is still being clarified

## Workshop / Early Paper

Use when:
- problem is real
- contribution is narrow
- artifact or evaluation is promising but still partial

Typical targets vary by community workshops attached to SOSP, OSDI, NSDI, SIGMOD, VLDB, FAST, and others.

## Mid-To-Strong Conference Band

Use when:
- there is a bounded but real contribution
- the artifact is solid
- evaluation is credible
- prior-art gap is clear

Examples:
- FAST
- USENIX ATC
- EuroSys
- ICDE

## Top-Tier Conference Band

Use when:
- the problem matters broadly
- the contribution changes a meaningful boundary
- evidence is unusually clean
- reviewers can clearly see why this is not an incremental variant

Examples:
- SOSP
- OSDI
- NSDI
- SIGMOD
- VLDB

## Protocol-Focused Band

Use when:
- the contribution is fundamentally about replicated-state or coordination semantics
- proof burden and model precision dominate the contribution

Examples:
- PODC
- DISC

## Anti-Pattern

Do not say "SOSP-level idea" when the artifact only supports a workshop claim.
Do not say "SIGMOD-level" when the data-management mechanism is thin and the result is mostly systems cleanup.
