# Kill Signals

Kill or pivot early when you see these patterns.

## Fake-Problem Signals

- nobody pays this cost in practice
- the issue vanishes once the boundary is drawn correctly
- the "problem" is mostly naming, taxonomy, or taste

## Prior-Art Signals

- one existing paper already owns nearly the same claim
- one industrial system already ships the core mechanism
- your main difference is scope, polish, or terminology

## Feasibility Signals

- no realistic baseline
- no access to required environment or data
- too much infrastructure must be built before the first result
- proof burden is larger than the engineering team can realistically carry

## Venue Signals

- strongest credible claim is much weaker than the intended venue bar
- evaluation only demonstrates a local engineering benefit
- the result is interesting internally but not yet community-interesting
